## What Is This?

You know how D&D works. You sit at a table with your friends, someone is the Dungeon Master, everyone has character sheets, pencils, erasers, dice, and a rulebook that weighs more than some of your characters.

And it's great. It really is. But let's be honest — there's a lot of friction:

- **"Wait, what's my modifier for Athletics again?"** — Flipping through a crumpled character sheet
- **"How many spell slots do I have left?"** — Erasing and rewriting the same circle for the 4th time
- **"Does Stunning Strike require a saving throw?"** — Spending 3 minutes finding the answer in the PHB
- **"When are we playing next?"** — A 47-message group chat that still doesn't have a clear answer
- **"We don't have a DM"** — The eternal problem

QuestForge solves all of this.

It's a **digital companion** that sits alongside your physical D&D sessions. You still meet in person. You still roleplay face-to-face. You still eat pizza and argue about whether that was a valid use of Misty Step. But instead of paper, pencils, and page-flipping — you have QuestForge.

And if you don't have a DM? QuestForge has an AI that can run the entire game for you.

Imagine the League of Legends client, but for D&D.

You log in. You see your character — a Level 7 Half-Orc Barbarian named Thorn Ironveil. His stats, his inventory, his spell list (well, he's a Barbarian, so there isn't one). Your friends are online. There's a session scheduled for tonight at 9 PM at the Guild gaming club.

That's QuestForge.

## Why Does This Exist?

### The Problem

D&D is the best collaborative storytelling game ever made. It's also stuck in a workflow designed in 1974.

Paper character sheets get lost, damaged, or become unreadable after too many eraser marks. Rules are scattered across expensive books that not everyone owns. Scheduling a session with 4-6 adults is harder than the actual dungeon. And finding a good Dungeon Master is like finding a legendary magic item — rare, valuable, and everyone wants one.

The tools that exist today don't actually solve this:

- **D&D Beyond** is great for reading rules and building characters online, but it's basically a digital book with a character builder. It doesn't help you at the table.
- **Roll20** and **Foundry VTT** are virtual tabletops designed for playing online. They replace the table entirely. If you want to sit with your friends, they're overkill.
- **Paper and pencils** work, but they're slow, error-prone, and don't scale.

None of these solutions address the core experience: sitting at a table with your friends and playing D&D without the friction.

### The Solution

QuestForge is built for one specific scenario: **you and your friends are at the same table, and you want to play D&D without dealing with paperwork.**

It handles:
- **Your character** — creation, stats, spells, inventory, leveling, all automated with D&D 5e rules
- **Your group** — friends list, chat, session scheduling, notifications
- **Your session** — dice rolling, initiative tracking, combat management, spell slot tracking
- **Your story** — AI Dungeon Master that can run games, assist human DMs, or voice NPCs
- **Your community** — guilds, event boards, local group discovery

And it does all of this while looking and feeling like a game client, not a web app.

## Core Features (Plain English)

### 🧙 Character Management

You create your character once. QuestForge knows all the D&D 5e rules — which races can be which classes, how ability scores work, what spells are available at each level, how much a longsword weighs.

The creation process is a guided wizard: pick your race, pick your class, assign ability scores (standard array, point buy, or roll 4d6 drop lowest — your choice), choose a background, buy equipment. At each step, the app explains what things mean and automatically calculates derived stats (modifiers, saving throws, skill bonuses, AC, initiative).

After creation, your character sheet is always current. Level up? The app walks you through new features, hit points, and spell choices. Used a spell slot? Tap to mark it. Took damage? Adjust HP with one tap. Picked up a magic sword? Add it to inventory and the app adjusts your attack bonus.

You can have multiple characters. Switch between them depending on which campaign you're playing.

### 👥 Friends & Social

Add your D&D friends. See who's online. Send messages. Create group chats for your party. This is basic social functionality, but it's important because it keeps your D&D social life in one place instead of scattered across Discord, WhatsApp, and text messages.

### 🎪 Lobbies & Session Scheduling

This is where the LoL inspiration is most obvious. Want to play? Create a lobby:

- Name your session ("The Sunken Temple, Part 3")
- Set a date and time
- Set a location ("Tbilisi, Table Games Club Guild")
- Choose your DM mode (human DM or AI DM)
- Invite friends or share a link

Everyone who's invited gets a notification. The app sends reminders as the session approaches. When it's time, everyone opens the lobby and the session begins.

No more "hey when are we playing" messages. No more confusion about where or when. The lobby is the source of truth.

### ⚔️ Live Session

When a session starts, QuestForge transforms. You get:

**Dice Roller:** Tap any die (d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, d20). The result appears with your relevant modifier automatically applied. Roll with advantage or disadvantage. Your party members can see your rolls in the session feed.

**Initiative Tracker:** Everyone rolls initiative (or the app auto-rolls for everyone). A clear turn order appears. When it's your turn, you get a notification. Everyone sees whose turn it is.

**Combat Manager:** Track HP for players and monsters. Apply damage, healing, temporary HP. Death saving throws. Conditions (poisoned, stunned, grappled, etc.) with automatic rule reminders — like "you have disadvantage on attack rolls while poisoned."

**Spell Tracker:** See your spell slots by level. Tap to use one. The app knows which spells require concentration and reminds you. When you rest, slots are recovered automatically based on rest type.

**Session Feed:** A running log of everything that happens — dice rolls, combat events, AI DM narration, player actions. After the session, this becomes your session recap.

### 🤖 AI Dungeon Master

This is QuestForge's signature feature. It works in several modes:

**Full DM Mode:** No human DM needed. The AI runs everything — narration, NPCs, encounters, combat management, story progression. It creates adventures, voices characters, describes environments, and responds to player actions. It knows D&D 5e rules perfectly and never makes calculation errors.

This mode is perfect for:
- Groups that don't have an experienced DM
- Spontaneous "let's play right now" sessions
- Learning D&D for the first time (the AI is infinitely patient)

**DM Assistant Mode:** A human DM runs the game, but the AI helps. It can:
- Look up rules instantly ("What happens when you grapple an invisible creature?")
- Generate balanced encounters on the fly ("I need 3 medium-difficulty encounters for a level 5 party")
- Track initiative and combat math while the DM focuses on storytelling
- Suggest NPC dialogue and personality when the DM is improvising

**NPC Dialogue Mode:** Players can talk directly to NPCs. The AI maintains each NPC's personality, knowledge, secrets, and speech patterns. Want to interrogate the suspicious tavern keeper? Have a conversation with them. The AI knows what the NPC knows and what they're hiding.

**Encounter Generator:** The AI creates balanced combat encounters based on party level, number of players, and difficulty preference. It selects appropriate monsters, designs terrain, and generates loot. Useful for DMs who need quick content.

**Rule Enforcer:** The AI watches the game and validates actions against D&D 5e rules. Did someone try to cast two leveled spells in one turn? The AI flags it. Did a player forget to add their proficiency bonus? The AI catches it. This mode can be turned on or off depending on how strict the group wants to be.

### 🏰 Guilds & Community

Guilds are groups of D&D players who organize together. A guild might be:
- A gaming cafe that hosts weekly D&D nights
- A university D&D club
- A group of friends who play multiple campaigns
- An online community that coordinates in-person meetups

Guilds have:

- An event calendar for scheduling sessions
- A "looking for players" board

You can discover guilds near you based on location. If you're in Tbilisi and want to find a D&D group, you can browse local guilds and join one.

## How a Typical Session Works

Let's walk through a real scenario:

### Before the Session

1. **Giorgi** creates a lobby: "Mines of Drakkenheim, Session 4" — Saturday at 7 PM, Table Games Club Guild in Tbilisi. He selects AI DM mode.
2. He invites **Nika**, **Mariam**, and **Dato**. They each get a push notification.
3. Everyone confirms. The lobby shows 4/4 players ready.
4. On Saturday at 6 PM, everyone gets a reminder: "Your session starts in 1 hour!"

### During the Session

5. Everyone arrives at the club, opens QuestForge, and joins the session.
6. The AI DM sets the scene: *"You stand before the crumbling entrance to the Mines of Drakkenheim. The air is thick with the smell of sulfur. Last session, you recovered the map from the goblin scouts. The main passage descends into darkness."*
7. **Nika** (playing Thorn, the Barbarian) says out loud: "I light a torch and head in first." He taps "Declare Action" in the app and types his action.
8. The AI DM responds with what Thorn sees inside.
9. Combat begins. Everyone rolls initiative (tap the d20 button — the app adds your DEX modifier). Turn order appears instantly.
10. On Thorn's turn, Nika uses his "Reckless Attack" ability (one tap). Rolls to hit (tap d20 — the app adds STR modifier + proficiency). It's a hit. Rolls damage (tap d12 — the app adds STR modifier + Rage bonus).
11. The AI DM describes the attack result and manages the enemy's HP.
12. This continues with full combat management — the app handles initiative order, HP tracking, spell slot usage, condition effects, death saves — everything.

### After the Session

13. The session ends. QuestForge generates a recap: all dice rolls, combat outcomes, story progression, XP earned, loot found.
14. Characters are automatically updated with new XP and loot.
15. The group schedules the next session in the lobby.

## What Makes QuestForge Different?

### vs D&D Beyond
D&D Beyond is a digital bookshelf and character builder. It's excellent for what it does, but it doesn't help you run a session. It doesn't have lobbies, session management, real-time combat tracking, or an AI DM. It's a reference tool. QuestForge is a game client.

### vs Roll20 / Foundry VTT
These are virtual tabletops designed for online play. They're built around maps, tokens, and video chat. If your group plays remotely, they're great. But if you're sitting at the same table, they're bloated with features you don't need (virtual maps, fog of war, token movement) and missing features you do need (scheduling, social features, AI DM).

### vs Paper and Pencils
Paper works. It's just slow, error-prone, and doesn't remember anything. QuestForge does everything paper does, faster, correctly, and with a complete history.

### The Real Difference
QuestForge is the only platform designed specifically for **in-person D&D sessions** with **AI capabilities**. It's not trying to be a virtual tabletop. It's not trying to be a digital book. It's a companion that makes your real-world game better.

## D&D 5e Rules — How Strict?

Very strict. QuestForge uses the **D&D 5th Edition System Reference Document (SRD)** as its canonical rules source. This means:

- All ability checks, attack rolls, and saving throws follow exact D&D 5e math
- All SRD races, classes, spells, items, and monsters are included
- Character creation follows RAW (Rules As Written)
- Combat mechanics (action economy, opportunity attacks, conditions) are implemented exactly
- There is no homebrew built in (though DM override allows flexibility)

The SRD is available under Creative Commons, so QuestForge can legally include this content. Content from paid sourcebooks (Xanathar's Guide, Tasha's Cauldron, etc.) is not included by default but could be added through content packs in the future.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**"Do I need to know D&D to use this?"**
No. QuestForge is designed to make D&D accessible. The character creation wizard explains everything. The AI DM teaches you as you play. The app handles all the math and rules.

**"Can I still use physical dice?"**
Absolutely. Roll your real dice and enter the result manually. QuestForge doesn't replace your dice — it just offers a digital alternative.

**"Does everyone need a phone/device?"**
Yes, each player needs a device (phone, tablet, or laptop) to access their character sheet and interact during the session.

**"Is the AI DM as good as a human DM?"**
Different, not necessarily better or worse. A human DM brings personal creativity, improvisation, and emotional intelligence that AI can't fully match. The AI DM brings perfect rules knowledge, instant encounter generation, infinite patience, and availability — it's always ready to play. Many groups will use both: AI DM for casual sessions, human DM for campaign climaxes.

**"Is this free?"**
The monetization model hasn't been finalized yet. The likely approach is freemium: basic features (character management, lobbies, dice rolling) are free. Premium features (AI DM, advanced analytics, expanded content) require a subscription.

**"Can I play online with this?"**
QuestForge is designed for in-person play, but the underlying technology (real-time sync, chat, session management) supports remote play too. Online mode is planned for a future update.

**"What about homebrew content?"**
The initial release is strictly D&D 5e SRD. Custom content support (homebrew races, classes, items, rules) is planned for a future update.

**"Can I import my existing D&D Beyond character?"**
This is a planned feature but not available at launch.
